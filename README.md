## What Is the Umm Al Qura Calendar?

The Umm Al Qura Calendar is a **calculated lunar calendar** adopted by Saudi Arabia
to determine Islamic dates such as Ramadan, Hajj, Eid holidays, and official
government records.

It is based on **astronomical calculations** performed for the geographical
location of Mecca, allowing Islamic dates to be determined and announced
in advance with consistency and reliability.

---

## Official Use

The Umm Al Qura Calendar is officially used in Saudi Arabia for:

- Determining the start of Islamic months
- Announcing Ramadan and Eid dates
- Scheduling the Hajj season
- Government and financial records
- Public holidays and national planning

Because of its official role, the Umm Al Qura Calendar is widely referenced
both inside and outside Saudi Arabia.

---

## How the Umm Al Qura Calendar Works

A new Hijri month in the Umm Al Qura Calendar begins when specific
astronomical conditions are met, including:

- The occurrence of the astronomical new moon (conjunction)
- The moon setting after the sun in Mecca
- The conjunction occurring before sunset

If these conditions are satisfied, the following day is designated
as the first day of the new Islamic month.

This method provides a standardized and predictable calendar system
while remaining aligned with Islamic principles.

---

## Umm Al Qura Calendar and Ramadan

Ramadan dates in Saudi Arabia are determined using the Umm Al Qura Calendar.
This allows fasting schedules, work hours, and religious events to be
announced in advance.

Due to differences in calendar methodologies, Ramadan dates based on
the Umm Al Qura Calendar may differ by one day from dates observed in
countries that rely on local moon sighting.
